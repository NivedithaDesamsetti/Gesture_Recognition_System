About / Help

MediaPipe is a framework developed by Google for building pipelines to process video, audio, and other multimedia. It is widely used for real-time computer vision tasks such as hand tracking, face detection, and pose estimation. In our app, we use MediaPipe Hands to track and interpret hand movements for sign language recognition.

Our app uses the MediaPipe Hands model to detect 21 key points on each hand in real time. These points are analyzed to recognize predefined gestures. A gesture classification algorithm maps these hand positions to specific sign language meanings, which are then translated into text and audio.

We aim to break communication barriers by providing a real-time sign language interpreter for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. Our goal is to make technology more inclusive, enabling easier interaction through gesture recognition and speech synthesis.
